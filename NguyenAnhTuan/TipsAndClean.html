SHOES'S LACES

Laces need just as much care as anything else. If you have white laces, normal wash them on cold (if washing in a machine, place in either a pillowcase or a "bra wash bag" made for women's delicates).

INSOLE ODOR

The most 'funked' up part of the shoe needs love, too. Sometimes itâ€™s a basic airing out, but if the odor is excessive, remove them and store them in an enclosed box with baking soda to eliminate the smell.

SUEDE

Yes, we know: Don't get suede wet. Luckily, since most sneakers are mass produced, they are easy to clean. A quick and easy under $10 solution is a suede eraser and brush (easily found online or at most shoe stores). The eraser allows you to 'rub' out stains and water spots while revitalizing the suede. Upon completion, brush away excess eraser dust.

OUTSOLE

I personally use a solution of 70% eco-friendly dish soap and 30% purified water to clean my outsoles. First, wet with a spray bottle, scrub with a brush, and then scrub soap with a dish soap dispenser brush until clean. Re-spray to remove dirt and grime, then towel or blow-dry (on cold). Always brush with the lines of your shoes' outsole. I also keep a small set of tools that is actually an iPhone tool kit to get any hard-to-reach areas.

EXCESSIVE CREASING

While everyone says to wear your kicks, some creasing can be excessive. To protect toe boxes after wear, I insert cedar shoe trees in conjunction with plastic sneaker shields. This lets the shoe resettle while it's stored.
